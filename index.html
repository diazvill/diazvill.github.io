Mississippi’s households experience the highest rate of energy insecurity in the entire country, with 4 out of every 10 households struggling to pay their energy bills. Census tracts with high and severe average household energy burdens are mostly concentrated in the Mississippi Delta.

Energy burdens faced by low- to moderate income (LMI) American Indian, Alaska Native, and Native Hawaiian households. Severe burdens are concentrated in Alaska. These disparities are not incidental, they reflect broader patterns of environmental injustice rooted in colonization, forced displacement, and federal policies that have systematically marginalized Native communities.
