Mississippi’s households experience the highest rate of energy insecurity in the entire country, with 4 out of every 10 households struggling to pay their energy bills. Census tracts with high and severe average household energy burdens are mostly concentrated in the Mississippi Delta region, bordering the Mississippi River to the west and the Yazoo River to the east, and the state’s rural communities.

Energy burdens faced by low- to moderate income (LMI) American Indian, Alaska Native, and Native Hawaiian households. The highest burdens are concentrated in Alaska due to high energy consumption, reliance on diesel generators, and limited grid connectivity. Many rural communities face high utility costs, reflecting broader patterns shaped by colonization, displacement, and systemic inequality.
